I am a Mechatronics and Robotics Engineering student at Ain Shams University, specialising in control systems, autonomous vehicles, and embedded engineering. My technical foundation spans PID-based vehicle control and UAV design through to biomedical device prototyping and ROS2-based autonomous navigation.

As a Control Engineer at the ASU Racing Team, I develop real-time control algorithms — including PID loops and path-tracking systems — for formula-style vehicles, directly applying academic theory to high-performance hardware in a competitive engineering environment.

I recently developed a fully autonomous vehicle controller for the Shell Urban Concept simulator, implementing Pure Pursuit steering control and PID speed regulation with ROS2 and Gazebo — achieving zero manual intervention across the track.

Leading R&D at EDU-X, I drive curriculum development and project prototyping across mechanical and embedded tracks. I am simultaneously designing a 10 kg fixed-wing UAV from aerodynamic analysis through flight controller development, integrating CFD simulation, stability modelling, and custom firmware.

My goal is to contribute to teams building intelligent autonomous systems — where precision control, embedded software, and mechanical design intersect.
